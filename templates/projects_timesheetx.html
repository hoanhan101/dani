Redesigning an Outdated Timesheet Website for Student Employees

TimesheetX is a comprehensive web solution for student employment timesheet needs. It is used and trusted by hundreds of higher education institutions and millions of students across the US today.

The Problem

Students use TimesheetX every other week to report time worked with supervisors to get paid. However, its student experience falls short of satisfactory. Students constantly report frustration and difficulty of use.

Diverging to discover

Kickoff

After getting an overview of TimesheetX, I set out to try it for myself to understand first-hand the struggles that my fellow students are facing. First, as an average human being and second, as a designer with a checklist of usability heuristics.

Usability audit reveals major issues in user control, consistency, and efficiency of use.

Besides the uninspiring and outdated UI, the website is an out-of-the-box solution without any information architecture or customizations to streamlined the timesheet workflow.

Insights from student employees

I sat down with five student employees to find answer to key questions:

  • What are students’ working and timesheet habits?
  • What are their pain points when reporting timesheets?
  • What do student employees need and want?

Students feel forced to do a tedious task on a difficult website and untended by their colleges. They often face limitations that requires workarounds with supervisors.

Although the students’ flow usually starts on the phone - reading email reminders of approaching timesheet submission deadlines, they have to switch to a laptop because TimesheetX’s mobile experience is too limited.

Even senior students struggle every time with navigation to locate their jobs and wanted timesheets. The website presents redundant features and information that often limit students’ control and require high cognitive load to skim through.

Insights from competitors

What other timesheet services are colleges using for student employments? After identifying TimesheetX’s competitors and reviewing them, I found a big gap in the field.
