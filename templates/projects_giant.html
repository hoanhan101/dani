Simplifying Crypto Investing for Beginners

Giant (pre-seed, early-stage startup) · Solo UX designer · 4 weeks

Context

Giant is a startup in stealth mode with a mission to simplify decentralized finance (DeFi) for beginners. Giant founders see a problem in the currently complicated crypto investing process that requires understanding and using multiple platforms.

*This is an ongoing project; this case study captures the early phase of designing the MVP.

Design process ↓

Insights from founders

To start, I talked to the startup founders to learn more about Giant, its mission, and any potential design and technical constraints we might have.

In short, Giant wants to be the middle-man that brings users the freedom and variety of decentralized exchanges (DEX) and the convenience and security of centralized exchanges (CEX). It also allows customers to track and earn interests from their investments in the app without using separate tracking and earning services.

From our early conversations, we established that our MVP would target beginner and intermediate buyers while focusing on 3 main stages of the investing process: buying, tracking, and earning. As a special request, the founders would like to design dark mode first!

Insights from users

I interviewed 5 users who fit our target audience to understand their views on investing and crypto. While all users only have one primary motivation, which is making money, they have 99 reasons stopping them from investing in crypto.

Meet the users

Supported by data from the user interviews, I developed two user personas as reference points for design decisions moving forward and assist the ongoing discussion with the stakeholders.

Christina is a crypto-newbie who needs a helping hand to start learning about crypto investing.

Eric is an intermediate retail investor who wants a hand-free investing and earning process.

MVP definition

Since more ideas have been evoked after research, we used a MoSCoW diagram to identify which ones will be in the scope of our MVP. During this phase of the product, we focus on the Must-have and Should-have.

We also agreed to have an educational feature in the final product and will continue to explore more ideas for it, though it would not be the focus for the MVP.

Information architecture

From the functionality scope, I created a high-level app map to visualize the placement of the main features.

After, I drafted our ideal user flows for the important sections: buying flow, track screens, and earn screens. We also discussed whether to design an onboarding flow during this phase. We eventually decided to experiment with waiting until after a few first usability tests to use the test results to guide our onboarding ideation.

Logo & Style

Giant was created with a mission to simplify crypto to empower everyone to grow their financial knowledge and personal wealth. So our inspiration for the brand design revolved around 3 keywords: growth, wealth, and friendliness.

Testing & Iteration

I tested the first MVP version with 6 users who fit the target audience. Overall, they responded well to the prototype and finished the given tasks. They also especially like the Resources feature. Nevertheless, there is still room for improvement. Here are some major changes we made:

1. Bottom navigation & transactions

Although we thought naming a tab “Buy” would provide a clear guide for users to access market assets, it confuses them about the other transactions (buy, deposit, and withdraw) whereabouts. So, we renamed it “Market” and presented transaction types more prominently.

2. Home screen

Instead of a short list of assets on the market, our users prefer to have prominent access to their holdings. They want to know how their investments perform the moment entering the app. So, we swap out the “Market” section for “Your investments.”

3. Buying flow

Since earning options require extensive research for beginners to understand, we decided not to let users decide. Instead, we will automatically update them with the best options and provide the freedom to turn on/off earnings.

Next steps

  • We will adopt a Nickel Tour and a Checklist for onboarding. The Nickel Tour provides a quick and straightforward introduction to Giant and how it works, while a checklist will be a direct guide for users to get started using the app.
  • Since users responded very positively to learning resources, we will focus more on this part and explore ideas to make learning fun! We have in mind a learning curriculum, taking quizzes and getting rewarded, and sharing learning experiences.

Stay tuned for Giant updates!
